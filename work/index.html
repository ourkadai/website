EZee Infotech

Ezee Infotech supports most of the privately-owned transport and logistics service providers in the South of India. They are the custodian of most of the critical business data pertaining to these service providers supporting various business aspects including online ticket booking, various operational metrics, and real-time tracking of the fleet. It is ably led and managed by Jawahar who is keen on taking the business to next level with a clear vision to be a leader in his industry leveraging technology to address the business challenges.
OurKadai’s founder members are avid readers and listeners of new technologies and OpenSource technologies enthusiasts. We represent ourselves in various tech meetups & conferences across South Asia and are active members of the Bangalore Open Java User Group (JUG). We volunteer in such conferences and present our thoughts, deliver tech sessions and publish papers on the topics of interest to the active communities.
It is in one such technical conference where we get to meet and greet Jawahar where we presented the concept of Zero Downtime Indexing (ZDI). Jawahar was keen to know more about ZDI as he was facing a unique challenge in managing the ever-growing size of the database which is used to manage the everyday business operation of his logistics and transport service providers. It is impossible for him to have any downtime of the database instance which is swelling in size and becoming a zombie. 
OurKadai worked along with Jawahar in understanding the challenges and thoughtfully provided the solutions to address the database issues by seamlessly migrating to a partitioned database with zero downtime impact to the business. Thorough planning and flawless execution enabled this migration possible and reinforced the faith Ezee Infotech instilled in our skillsets.
It’s not a one-night stand affair. We explained SDG team about various facades of online business platforms by breaking them into easily conceivable vignettes. At the end of that discussion, we clarified numerous qualms that were blocking SDG’s online expedition. We could observe happy expressions in SDG’s team where we sensed the trust started budding amongst.
The team brainstormed diverse technical options and finally short-listed a couple of approaches that suit SDG’s business model where every aspect of the foreseeable future challenges was explained to SDG to enable them in deciding the right approach. Prototyping was completed and Proof of Concepts was presented that showed how exactly the solution would be in play post-implementation.
Right from front-ending website to the back office support, inventory control and Accounting aspects were also constituted in the solution that persisted as the most cost-effective approach in long run with bare minimal technical support that would be required for an ongoing business concern.
